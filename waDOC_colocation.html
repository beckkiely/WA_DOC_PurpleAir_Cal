PM2.5 Channel Agreement: Individual Sensors for 5/20-6/1

To examine whether the channels agree with each other on individual sensors, I created plots of channel a vs. channel b., and included a regression line and the R^2 value.

plot regression of channel a vs b

Wow, more issues on this batch than typical. Unit f0:24:f9:c6:91:c0 has an R^2 of 0.59, it looks like many of the points are along a good trend, so I’m interested in looking at a time series of the readings and see if one sensor went bad at a certain point through the colocation.

Next problematic unit is f0:24:f9:c6:c8:85, which has about 166 readings on sensor b that are well over 250ug/m3, with many nearing 3500 ug/m3. This was a string of bad readings from 5/23 at 0601 to 1126. Even if we filter out all readings over 250ug/m3, we get an R^2 of 0.59, so definetely some big issues there.

Unit f0:24:f9:c6:e4:9e has an R^2 of 0.9, which is lower than typical, and some visible deviations from the A channel. These deviations center around 5/23 at 0526, which is about a half hour before one of the channels on a different unit went off the rails… interesting. EXCLUDE THIS TIME PERIOD AND RERUN??

Otherwise, the 8 remaining units have R^2 from 0.9 to 0.96, with many of them tending to be more around the 0.92 range. Not the best, but they should be fine.

time series of channel a vs b

Looking at unit f0:24:f9:c6:91:c0, it does look like after 5/23 at about 0600 it started having massive random spikes on channel A, while otherwise being mostly well correlated. With these spikes happening at more or less random, I don’t think it can be reliably used.

Here’s also an additional time series for the unit with crazy high readings, but with those filtered out. Still looking like the A channel is having some issues.
